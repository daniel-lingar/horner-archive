## OQ-12 — William Gordon Acord — CLOSED

### Finding

| Field | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Name | William Gordon Acord (also William G. Acord) |
| NARA M317 card | **#44903963** |
| Rank | Private |
| Unit | **Co. H, 10th Arkansas Militia (CSA)** |
| Death | **18 March 1864**, Little Mulberry Creek, Johnson County |
| Burial | Yale Cemetery, Oark, Johnson County |

**Resolves the 11th Arkansas Infantry question:** Index and secondary corroboration place him in the **10th Arkansas Militia**, not the 11th Arkansas Infantry.

### Corroborating sources

1. **Dossier v2 / M317 index extract** — card #44903963, Co. H, 10th Arkansas Militia [PRIMARY/HIGH].
2. **United Daughters of the Confederacy** — Interment #113770: Pvt, Co. H, **10th Militia**, AR; b. 1827, d. 3/18/1864, Yale Cemetery, Johnson County.  
   https://hqudc.org/interments/acord-william-g/
3. **Fahnstrom 2000** (*Francis Marion Acord*, ARGenWeb) — Acord brothers mustered with **10th Regiment Arkansas militia at Clarksville** in early 1864 before dispersing to regular units.
4. **Genealogy Trails — Yale Cemetery** — `W. G. Acord, b. 1827; d. 1864; CO K 2ND AR INF VOL, CIVIL WAR` (tombstone inscription; Union claim).
5. **Family pattern** — Brother Francis Marion Acord enlisted **Co. K, 2nd Arkansas Infantry (US)** 24 Jan 1864 at Clarksville. Gordon's tombstone reflects the **intended** Union path; he was murdered **before** confirmed Union muster.

### Union service note

Tombstone and Yale transcription claim **Co. K, 2nd Arkansas Infantry (US)**. No Union CMSR for William Gordon Acord has been retrieved in this archive. Treat tombstone as **family memorial claim**, not proved primary service.
