SECTION 19 & 20 — North line between Sections 19 & 20
Date: January 5, 1844 (Book 913A page 17 / COSL seq 17)

Between Sections 19 & 20:
  20.30  Entered John Arringtons field brs E & W & here entered bottom brs E & W
  20.25  Left the field brs E & W
  20.25  West Mulberry distance obtained by calculation 144 links wide runs West
  39.06  Entered G. Homers field brs E & W
  40.00  Set a ¼ Section corner post [witness trees: Black Gum/Walnut 14 in, Black Oak 14 in]
  58.40  Left the field brs East & West
  60.00  A Road brs East & West & here left the bottom brs E & W
  64.30  A Brook 20 links wide runs S W
  80.00  Set a post corner to Sections 17,18,19 & 20

Land: "hilly & Rocky except the bottom which is 1st rate soil timber Oak Gum &c undergrowth Oak bushes &c"

Archive note: "G. Homers field" is a surveyor's 1844 occupation notation on the river bottom — not proved as Spencer Horner (patent assignee 1857) without deed chain. Phonetic Homer/Horne/Horner variants appear in census for this family.

RELEVANCE TO HORNER HOMESTEAD (Patent 19B, 160 acres)

Patent parcels: Sec 19 (NE¼ SE¼, NW¼ SE¼) · Sec 22 (NW¼ NW¼, SE¼ NW¼)

1844–1845 surveyors describe:
  • River bottom ("1st rate soil") vs upland ("hilly & Rocky")
  • Named watercourses: Mulberry (110–144 links wide), multiple brooks
  • Roads crossing section lines
  • Pre-patent fields: Arrington, G. Homer(s), Subanks

Corroborates oral/LiDAR geography: bluff-upland vs bottom-land contrast along Little Mulberry corridor. Does not name Spencer Horner (b. ~1809) — he acquires via Choctaw scrip in 1857.
