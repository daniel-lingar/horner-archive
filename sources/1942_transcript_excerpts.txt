The following reconstruction draws on the 1942 Eva Horner family history (FindAGrave #9297073 — the primary source document for the attack), FamilySearch record LH23-VP3 (HeatherAcord notes), and the Larry Kraus / Annie Everette family history shared with Rhonda Hawkins pre-2000.

From the 1942 Eva Horner transcript: "Dad said his horse needed shoeing and he had to go out on the mountain 5 miles to get it shod. He told his brother, Will, and his father Spencer Horner that whatever they did, never go to the house in the daytime. But the mother was killing a chicken so the father Spencer Horner and Will went to the house."

From the 1942 transcript: "Wasn't long till about 20 rebels came riding up. Grandfather went out into the potato patch and said, 'I surrender!' and they shot him down."

From the 1942 transcript: "Will ran through the lot and up on the mountain side and was shot in the head."

"Wm. R. Homer Born Aug. 1, 1834 Killed Aug. 10, 1864 by parties known to his family."

The misspelling of "Horner" as "Homer" is in the original inscription. The phrase "by parties known to his family" is public accusation cut in stone by people without legal recourse.

From the 1942 transcript: "Grandfather had a pot of gold and silver and paper money, but they never did find it after he was killed. Louis had a dream about 'a black gum tree in the middle of a field — put my back to the tree, took ten steps toward the house, dug down, and found the pot of gold.'"

Tombstone: "Wm. R. Homer Born Aug. 1, 1834 Killed Aug. 10, 1864 by parties known to his family."

The 1942 family oral history (as told by his son Prof. John Turner Horner) states he was conscripted by the Confederates, then "managed to get away, slipped out on them and joined the Union Army" and recruited Company K. The FindAGrave biographical note disputes this, stating there is no record of Union service — the basis for the rejection of his 1893 pension application.

CONFLICT: The 1942 oral history says he was conscripted, deserted, and recruited Company K. The FindAGrave biographical note says he was never conscripted — only served in the militia — and never formed Company K (no record exists). Both accounts are in the same source document. The pension rejection is documented fact. The truth of his Union service is unresolved.

The Revenge Action — From the 1942 Transcript

The following is from the 1942 Eva Horner family history, told by Prof. John Turner Horner to his daughter Eva. It is second-generation oral tradition — the Doctor's son recounting what his father told him. It is cited as: "1942 Horner family history, recorded by Eva Horner from Prof. John Turner Horner, attributed to Dr. John Turner Horner."

The 1942 Eva Horner family history is the sole source for the revenge action. It is second-generation oral tradition transcribed into written record, preserved in FindAGrave Memorial #9297073. It is cited as such and graded accordingly.

Three operations: Cornfield ambush, road killing, dance house raid. All from the 1942 transcript.

Attack date: August 2, 1864. Multiple FamilySearch contributors; 1942 transcript.

Spencer shot in the potato patch while surrendering. 1942 transcript (verbatim); FamilySearch LH23-VP3.

William hidden in cave; GPS coordinates documented. 1942 transcript; FamilySearch; on-site visit 2003.

Spencer had hidden gold and silver never recovered. 1942 transcript (verbatim); FamilySearch notes.

Fate Arnold was wounded on an ammunition run; recovered at Marshfield; later found as an old man in Stone County. 1942 transcript.

Jess Wilson held the horses during combat operations. 1942 transcript.

Dr. John Turner Horner commanded a revenge squad that killed 17 of 20 bushwhackers. 1942 Eva Horner transcript. Three operations described with tactical specificity.

Dr. Horner's Confederate service: 1942 oral history says conscripted and deserted. FindAGrave biographical note says never conscripted — only served in militia that disbanded. Both in FindAGrave #9297073.

John Fry Hill's brother was Joseph Hill. Joseph Hill's daughter was Amanda Catherine Hill, who married John Samuel Acord — one of the two orphaned boys present at the farm on August 2, 1864, when Spencer was shot.

Marcus Mark Hill and Rachel Fry → two sons: Joseph Hill (Daniel's direct ancestor) and John Fry Hill (his brother, the Colonel and Sheriff) → Joseph's daughter Amanda Catherine Hill → married John Samuel Acord → Rosie Acord Sexton → Argie Faye Sexton Williams → Patricia Lynn Williams → Daniel Bret Lingar.
