Dear National Archives Reference Staff,

I am an independent researcher documenting civilian-targeted violence in Johnson County, Arkansas, during the Civil War. I respectfully request copies of the following federal records for scholarly and public-history use (non-commercial, with attribution).

### 1. OQ-15 — PRIORITY

**Record Group:** 15 (Records of the Veterans Administration / Bureau of Pensions)  
**Series:** Civil War and Later Pension Files  
**Applicant:** John Turner Horner  
**Application / Certificate Number:** 1,143,763  
**Filed:** March 2, 1894, McDonald County, Missouri  
**Disposition:** Rejected October 5, 1894 (claimed 2nd Lieutenant, Co. K, 2nd Arkansas Infantry, US; War Department ruled he recruited but did not formally enlist)

I am seeking the complete pension file, including sworn statements, witness affidavits, and War Department correspondence. This file is not available in full on Fold3 or Ancestry — only index cards have been reviewed.

### 2. OQ-16 — Bundle with above

**Record Group:** 94 (Records of the Adjutant General's Office)  
**Document:** Special Order B.O. 276-1866 (July 3, 1866)  
**Subject:** Removal of desertion charge against **Hiram Boen**, Company E, 2nd Arkansas Infantry (US)  
**Context:** Boen was AWOL from approximately May 28, 1864 through January 1, 1865 — the period of the August 1864 murders of Union-aligned civilians in Floyd Township, Johnson County, Arkansas, including John Spencer Horner and William Riley Horner.

If the Special Order is not locatable as a standalone item, please advise on the correct entry point in RG 94 for War Department special orders clearing desertion charges for this regiment and date.
