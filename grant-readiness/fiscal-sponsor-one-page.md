# Horner Civil War Archive — One-Page Fiscal Sponsor Case Statement

## Project Summary

The **Horner Civil War Archive** is a descendant-led public history project preserving and interpreting the Civil War-era violence, displacement, land history, and community memory surrounding the Horner, Hill, and Acord families of Johnson County, Arkansas.

The project centers on the August 1864 killing of John Spencer Horner and the broader family and community crisis surrounding Civil War guerrilla violence in Floyd Township. It uses verified sources, land records, military records, oral history, maps, cemetery evidence, and digital documentation to turn a local family tragedy into an accessible public history resource.

## Why It Matters

This project preserves a form of history that is often lost: the experience of ordinary rural families caught between armies, guerrilla violence, divided loyalties, and postwar memory. It is not only a genealogy project. It is a public humanities project about violence, survival, land, memory, and reconciliation in rural Arkansas.

The archive can support:

- Local history education
- Digital humanities work
- Civil War and Reconstruction research
- K-12 and university teaching
- Heritage tourism and site interpretation
- Cemetery and land-history preservation
- Descendant and community engagement

## Why Now

With the right partner, the Horner Civil War Archive can become a durable public history resource for Johnson County and Arkansas Civil War memory.
