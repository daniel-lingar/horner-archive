Primary Sources — On File

Land Patent 19B (April 1, 1857)

BLM GLO accession AR2690__.426 · Fayetteville Land Office · T12N R24W Primary

Establishes Spencer Horner as assignee of Co-cha-tubbee for 160 acres in Floyd Township. Foundation for understanding what property was at stake in 1864. On-site image →

Compiled Military Service Record — William Riley Horner, Co. K, 2nd Arkansas Infantry (US)

NARA RG 94 · Fold3 images 203236710, 203236689 Primary

Documents AWOL status at death and report "killed by guerrillas 10 August, 1864, Johnson Co., Arkansas." Government record of familial identification of killers.

NARA Pension Certificate WC75885 — William R. Horner

Widow Mary Ann Horner declarations 1865, 1885 Primary

Contains Maude Lily Horner Sturges account (page 3) — potato patch, cave refuge, Mary Ann nursing. Transcript on file; physical retrieval is OQ-funded work. §16 summary →

Moore Cemetery Tombstones — Spencer, William Riley, and Permelia Horner

GPS documented · FamilySearch LH23-VP3 Primary

William's stone accuses "parties known to his family." Field gallery published v20.1. Field documentation →

John Fry Hill — CMSR and Amnesty Petition (Case 4225)

7th Arkansas Cavalry (CSA) · NARA/Fold3 Primary

September 1864 "Absent — Detached" notation; 1866 petition claims late return to Johnson County — contradicted by military record. Establishes sheriff's wartime posture.

Hiram Boen CMSR — Co. E, 2nd Arkansas Infantry (US)

NARA M399 · 13 cards examined March 2026 Primary

AWOL May 1864 – January 1865; desertion charge cleared July 1866 without stated reason. Same company as Wash Middleton.

1860 Federal Census — Floyd Township, Johnson County

NARA M653 · pp. 981–982 Primary · imaged v20.5

John T. "Homer": $200 real / $1,000 personal. W.R. "Horne": $900 real. Published image · OQ-21 closed.

John Turner Horner — Rejected Invalid Pension #1,143,763

NARA RG 15 · Filed March 2, 1894; rejected October 5, 1894 Primary · not retrieved

Highest-priority unexamined file. Expected sworn statements on recruitment and post-war activity. OQ-15.

Oral & Family Sources

1942 Horner Family Oral History Transcript

Eva Horner, from Prof. John Turner Horner · FindAGrave #9297073 Oral

Names reprisal squad (Wash Middleton, Fate Arnold, Jess Wilson); describes attack, cave, gold, and "17 of 20" reprisal. Excerpts published →

Secondary & Regional Sources

Goodspeed, History of Barry County, Missouri (1888)

Secondary

Biographical entry for John Fry Hill — sheriff and Confederate colonel. Supports his public role but is not a criminal investigation record.

Yale Cemetery Transcription (Fahnstrom / ARGenWeb)

Secondary · tombstone data Primary

Documents Hill, Stewart, and Acord burials in same ground — geographic convergence of case actors.

Bittersweet — "From Hills to Hotels" (Summer 1977)

Shepherd of the Hills Farm research Secondary

Names Wash Middleton as first physical model for fictional Wash Gibbs; combines with Bald Knobber May for character menace.

Newton County AR Blog — "Wash and Jim" (January 2024)

Draws on Vincent Anderson, Bald Knobbers Secondary

Snapp killing (1886), jailbreak, 1888 death at Parthenon picnic. Useful for postwar Middleton context; not evidence for August 1864.

Historiography — Ozark Civil War (Context reading)

Daniel E. Sutherland, A Savage Conflict: The Devastating Civil War (2009)

Secondary · scholarly

Macro framework for guerrilla violence and civilian targeting in the trans-Mississippi theater. Useful for placing Johnson County in national historiography.
