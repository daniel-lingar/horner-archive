Project Summary

The Horner Civil War Archive is a descendant-led public history project preserving and interpreting the Civil War-era violence, displacement, land history, and community memory surrounding the Horner, Hill, and Acord families of Johnson County, Arkansas.

The project centers on the August 1864 killing of John Spencer Horner and the broader family and community crisis surrounding Civil War guerrilla violence in Floyd Township. It uses verified sources, land records, military records, oral history, maps, cemetery evidence, and digital documentation to turn a local family tragedy into an accessible public history resource.

Why It Matters

This project preserves a form of history that is often lost: the experience of ordinary rural families caught between armies, guerrilla violence, divided loyalties, and postwar memory. It is not only a genealogy project. It is a public humanities project about violence, survival, land, memory, and reconciliation in rural Arkansas.

The archive can support:

  • Local history education
  • Digital humanities work
  • Civil War and Reconstruction research
  • K-12 and university teaching
  • Heritage tourism and site interpretation
  • Cemetery and land-history preservation
  • Descendant and community engagement

Current Assets

  • Public GitHub archive and GitHub Pages website
  • Dossier and source-based narrative materials
  • KML/GIS mapping files and interactive site map
  • Historic land and patent material (including Choctaw scrip Patent 19B)
  • Field documentation — Moore Cemetery photographs (June 2026)
  • Census and genealogy evidence with open-question tracking
  • Grant-readiness and fiscal sponsor packet
  • Direct descendant leadership in Johnson County, Arkansas

The Ask

The Horner Civil War Archive is seeking a fiscal sponsor and early institutional partners to help move the project from personal research archive to sustainable public history initiative.

Requested support may include fiscal sponsorship for grants and donations, letters of support, advisory review, public program partnership, archival guidance, student intern or faculty partnership, and help identifying funders and community stakeholders.

First 12-Month Goal

  1. Formalize fiscal sponsorship and advisory support.
  2. Improve the public website and archive navigation.
  3. Build a small set of educational and public-facing materials.
  4. Host at least one public presentation or local history event.
  5. Submit early grant applications.
  6. Prepare the path toward independent nonprofit status if appropriate.

Why Now

The core research asset already exists. The next step is not starting from scratch. The next step is building the structure around it: fiscal sponsorship, local legitimacy, grant readiness, and public programming.
