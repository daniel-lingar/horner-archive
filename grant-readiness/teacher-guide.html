Purpose

This archive documents civilian-targeted violence in Johnson County, Arkansas, during August 1864 — a case where local law enforcement did not investigate killings of Union-aligned neighbors. The materials teach students how historians weigh primary documents, oral history, and silence in the record.

Recommended Audiences

  • Arkansas high school U.S. History (Civil War and Reconstruction units)
  • Arkansas Studies / local history electives
  • College introductory public history or historical methods courses
  • Genealogy and historical society adult education programs

Essential Questions

  1. How did guerrilla violence differ from formal battle in the Arkansas Ozarks?
  2. What can a land patent, census page, or muster roll prove — and what can it not prove?
  3. When oral family history and government records disagree, how should a historian proceed?
  4. Why might a community leave murders unprosecuted even when victims were "known to the family"?
  5. How do descendant researchers responsibly publish painful family history?

45-Minute Lesson Outline (Draft)

Opening (10 min): Map Johnson County; locate Floyd Township, Moore Cemetery, Yale Cemetery using the interactive site map.

Source lab (20 min): Compare three tiers on one event (August 2, 1864 attack):

  • Primary: William Riley Horner CMSR line ("killed by guerrillas 10 August, 1864")
  • Primary / moderate: Maude Lily Horner Sturges account in NARA WC75885 (§16)
  • Oral: 1942 transcript excerpt — children yelling "Here they come, Grandpap!"

Discussion (10 min): Sheriff John Fry Hill — conflict of interest when civil authority and Confederate command overlap.

Closing (5 min): Students list one verified fact, one inference, and one open question (from §13 OQ register).

Source-Tier Vocabulary

Teach students the archive's tier labels before assigning reading:

  • Primary: Original document or artifact from the period (patent, CMSR, tombstone)
  • Oral / Corroborated: Family tradition with partial verification
  • Secondary: Later account citing primary methodology (Goodspeed 1888; cemetery transcriptions)

Extension Activities (Funded expansion)

  • Trace the Horner–Hill–Acord–Stewart network using the Yale Cemetery convergence table
  • Debate: Is the reprisal "17 of 20" claim publishable without court records?
  • Compare fictional Wash Gibbs (Shepherd of the Hills) to documented Wash Middleton — legend vs. evidence
  • Field documentation exercise: what would students photograph at Moore Cemetery to verify a tombstone claim?
