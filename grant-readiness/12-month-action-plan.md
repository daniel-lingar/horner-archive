# Horner Civil War Archive — 12-Month Action Plan

## Purpose

This action plan gives a fiscal sponsor or partner a clear picture of what the Horner Civil War Archive can accomplish in its first year as a structured public history project.

The goal is not to overbuild. The goal is to create a credible foundation: sponsorship, advisory support, public-facing archive improvements, educational materials, local programming, and first grant submissions.

---

## Months 1–3: Foundation and Sponsorship

### Objectives

- Secure fiscal sponsor or formal partner.
- Create basic governance/advisory structure.
- Review existing archive assets.
- Set public history and source standards.

### Deliverables

- Signed fiscal sponsorship or partnership agreement.
- Advisory list of 3–5 people or organizations.
- Confirmed project description and mission language.
- Updated public website introduction.
- Simple intake process for new sources, images, stories, and corrections.

### Success Metrics

- Fiscal sponsor or lead partner identified.
- At least two letters of support requested or secured.
- Website and GitHub archive updated with grant-readiness materials.
- First advisory conversation completed.

---

## Months 4–6: Digital Archive and Education Buildout

### Objectives

- Improve archive navigation.
- Create public-facing educational materials.
- Organize source files and research notes.
- Build a small but usable digital humanities foundation.

### Deliverables

- Improved site navigation and landing page.
- Source index or bibliography page.
- Timeline page or downloadable timeline.
- Simple map/GIS page or linked KML resources.
- 1–2 draft educational handouts or lesson outlines.
- Internal file organization plan.

### Success Metrics

- Public site easier to navigate.
- At least one educational resource drafted.
- At least one source index or research guide created.
- Archive files organized into permanent folders.

---

## Months 7–9: Public Programming and Grant Preparation

### Objectives

- Begin public outreach.
- Prepare early grant applications.
- Build local credibility.
- Identify first public event or presentation.

### Deliverables

- One public talk, library program, classroom visit, or recorded presentation.
- Finalized small grant budget.
- Draft grant narrative.
- Partner support letters.
- Outreach list of local stakeholders.
- Basic press/community announcement language.

### Success Metrics

- At least one public program scheduled or completed.
- At least one grant application prepared or submitted.
- At least two partner conversations completed.
- Support letters or advisory statements collected.

---

## Months 10–12: Review, Stabilize, and Prepare Year 2

### Objectives

- Review Year 1 results.
- Decide whether to continue under fiscal sponsor or begin independent nonprofit formation.
- Prepare next grant cycle.
- Expand partnerships and community support.

### Deliverables

- Year 1 summary report.
- Updated budget for Year 2.
- Updated grant roadmap.
- List of needed volunteers, advisors, interns, or contractors.
- Decision memo on 501(c)(3) timing.

### Success Metrics

- Year 1 impact summarized.
- At least one grant submitted or ready for submission.
- Partner/fiscal sponsor relationship assessed.
- Clear Year 2 plan created.

---

## First-Year Priorities

1. Keep the project source-based and credible.
2. Build local trust before asking for major funding.
3. Start with modest public programming.
4. Improve digital access without overcomplicating the site.
5. Use fiscal sponsorship to unlock funding and legitimacy.
6. Avoid overpromising site access, tourism activity, or formal preservation work before permissions and partnerships are secured.

---

## End-of-Year Outcome

By the end of the first 12 months, the Horner Civil War Archive should be positioned as a credible, partner-backed, grant-ready public history initiative with an improved digital archive, early educational materials, local support, and a realistic path toward sustainability.
