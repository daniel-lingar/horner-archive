# Horner Civil War Archive — Partner Target List

## Purpose

This document identifies likely early partners for the Horner Civil War Archive and defines what to ask each one for. The goal is to secure fiscal sponsorship, letters of support, advisory help, and public programming partners before pursuing larger grants.

---

## Priority 1: Johnson County Historical Society

### Why They Matter

The project is rooted in Johnson County, Arkansas. A local historical society can provide legitimacy, community connection, local memory, and a natural public-history home.

### Possible Roles

- Fiscal sponsor
- Letter of support
- Public program host
- Local advisor
- Connection to community historians and descendants
- Help identifying local sites, records, and stakeholders

### First Ask

Request a short meeting to introduce the archive and ask whether they would consider fiscal sponsorship, advisory support, or a letter of support.

---

## Priority 2: University of the Ozarks

### Why They Matter

A university partner can strengthen the project academically and may offer faculty review, student involvement, public history support, or institutional credibility.

### Possible Roles

- Academic partner
- Fiscal sponsor or institutional partner
- Faculty advisor
- Student interns
- Digital humanities support
- Public presentation venue
- Letter of support

### First Ask

Request a conversation with a history, humanities, public history, library, or community engagement contact about student research and advisory partnership.

---

## Priority 3: Arkansas State Archives

### Why They Matter

The archive can benefit from source guidance, preservation advice, and credibility from a professional archival partner.

### Possible Roles

- Archival guidance
- Source review
- Preservation advice
- Referral to records and collections
- Letter of support
- Public access recommendations

### First Ask

Ask for guidance on source organization and whether the project may qualify for archival support, collaboration, or a support letter.

---

## Priority 4: Johnson County / Clarksville Public Library or Regional Library Partner

### Why They Matter

Libraries are strong public access partners. They can help turn the archive into community programming and educational access.

### Possible Roles

- Public talk host
- Genealogy workshop host
- Community access point
- Local history event partner
- Display or exhibit space
- Letter of support
- Possible fiscal sponsor referral

### First Ask

Ask whether the library would host an introductory public program or help connect the archive with local history/genealogy audiences.

---

## Priority 5: Arkansas Humanities Council or Regional Humanities Partner

### Why They Matter

The project is a public humanities project: rural history, Civil War memory, family history, source interpretation, and community education.

### Possible Roles

- Grant guidance
- Funding pathway
- Public humanities review
- Program design input
- Connection to other partners
- Support for talks, exhibits, or educational materials

### First Ask

Ask what fiscal sponsorship or partner structure would make the project eligible for early public humanities support.

---

## Priority 6: Local Government / Tourism / Heritage Stakeholders

### Why They Matter

Long-term site interpretation, signage, heritage tourism, and public access may eventually require local-government, tourism, or landowner coordination.

### Possible Roles

- Letter of support
- Tourism connection
- Site interpretation guidance
- Public access coordination
- Local promotion
- Long-term heritage planning

### First Ask

Do not start with a large tourism request. Start by asking for a general letter of support or informal guidance after the fiscal sponsor pathway is clearer.

---

## Outreach Order

1. Johnson County Historical Society
2. University of the Ozarks
3. Public library / regional library contact
4. Arkansas State Archives
5. Arkansas Humanities Council / humanities partner
6. Local government or tourism contact
