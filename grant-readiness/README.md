## Why Fiscal Sponsorship First?

Right now the project has:

- Strong, primary-source research (dossier v20.0)
- Public website + GitHub archive
- Clear historical and educational value
- Local Arkansas roots and descendant leadership

What it still needs:

- Legal nonprofit pathway
- Credibility and local legitimacy
- Ability to receive grants and tax-deductible donations
- Letters of support
- Advisory input and community connections
- Grant-ready structure and modest startup budget

A fiscal sponsor provides the fastest on-ramp without the immediate cost and complexity of forming an independent 501(c)(3).

---

## How to Use This Packet

1. Customize the documents with current partner names and contact information.
2. Send the **One-Page Case Statement** + **Request Letter** to target organizations.
3. Attach or link the full dossier and website.
4. Follow up with a short conversation or meeting.
5. Once a fiscal sponsor is secured, use the 12-Month Action Plan and Startup Budget to move into implementation and early grant applications.

---

## Target First Partners

1. **Johnson County Historical Society** — Local legitimacy, possible fiscal sponsorship or strong letter of support.
2. **University of the Ozarks** — Academic partnership, student interns, faculty review, possible institutional support.
3. **Arkansas State Archives** — Archival credibility, source guidance, possible in-kind support or letters.
4. **Johnson County / Clarksville Public Library or regional library system** — Public programming partner and community access point.
5. **Arkansas Humanities Council or regional humanities partners** — Grant pathway, public humanities alignment, and credibility.

See `partner-target-list.md` for detailed rationale and specific asks.

---

## Next Steps After Securing a Fiscal Sponsor

- Finalize the 12-month budget and action plan with sponsor input.
- Submit first small planning or implementation grants.
- Begin website enhancement, educational content development, and community outreach.
- Recruit a small advisory board.
- Move toward independent 501(c)(3) status within 12–18 months if desired.
