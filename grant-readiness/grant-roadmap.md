# Horner Civil War Archive — Grant Roadmap

## Purpose

This roadmap explains how the Horner Civil War Archive can move from personal research project to grant-ready public history initiative.

The correct first step is not a large grant application. The correct first step is fiscal sponsorship, partner support, and proof of readiness.

---

## Phase 1: Fiscal Sponsor and Partner Foundation

**Timing:** Months 1–3

### Goals

- Secure fiscal sponsor or institutional partner.
- Obtain letters of support.
- Confirm advisory structure.
- Finalize first-year budget and action plan.
- Improve project language for funders.

### Best Funding Targets

- Local donations
- Small local sponsorships
- Fiscal sponsor seed support
- In-kind support
- Small planning grants if eligible

### Key Deliverables

- Fiscal sponsorship agreement
- One-page case statement
- Startup budget
- Support letters
- Public website link
- Advisory list

---

## Phase 2: Public Humanities and Digital Access Grants

**Timing:** Months 4–9

### Goals

- Improve the public website and digital archive.
- Build educational materials.
- Create public programming.
- Document local impact.

### Best Funding Targets

- Public humanities grants
- State humanities council opportunities
- Local history grants
- Digital access support
- Library or education program support

### Possible Grant Uses

- Website navigation and accessibility
- Public presentation materials
- Source index and research guide
- Timeline and maps
- Lesson outlines
- Community event support

---

## Phase 3: Preservation, Education, and Interpretation

**Timing:** Months 9–18

### Goals

- Develop deeper educational and interpretive materials.
- Explore signage, site interpretation, or guided history work only after permissions are clear.
- Build partner-backed preservation strategy.

### Best Funding Targets

- Historic preservation planning grants
- Local heritage grants
- Education grants
- Museum/library programming grants
- Battlefield or Civil War interpretation opportunities where applicable

### Possible Grant Uses

- Interpretive signage concepts
- Archival preservation
- Oral history collection
- Teacher materials
- Public exhibit planning
- Community workshops

---

## Phase 4: Long-Term Sustainability

**Timing:** Year 2 and beyond

### Goals

- Decide whether to remain fiscally sponsored or form independent 501(c)(3).
- Build donor and membership base.
- Create repeatable public programming.
- Expand beyond the Horner focal case into broader Arkansas guerrilla-war and rural Civil War history.

### Revenue Mix

- Grants
- Donations
- Memberships
- Public programs
- Educational products
- Speaking engagements
- Local sponsorships
- In-kind support

---

## Grant Readiness Checklist

Before applying for larger grants, the project should have:

- Fiscal sponsor or legal applicant
- Clear mission and project scope
- Public website
- Organized source archive
- Budget
- Timeline
- Letters of support
- Partner list
- Advisory structure
- Public benefit statement
- Accessibility plan
- Sustainability plan

---

## First Grant Strategy

The first grant should be small and achievable. It should fund planning, public access, website improvement, educational materials, or a public program.

Avoid starting with a large capital, land, tourism, or monument request. Those can come later after partner support, permissions, and public legitimacy are in place.

---

## Best First Grant Narrative

The strongest first narrative is:

> A descendant-led Arkansas public history project has already built a source-based digital archive around Civil War guerrilla violence in Johnson County. With fiscal sponsorship and modest startup support, the project will improve public access, create educational materials, and hold community programming so this difficult local history can be preserved and shared responsibly.

---

## Bottom Line

The Horner Civil War Archive is already research-rich. The next funding step is structure: fiscal sponsor, local partners, advisory support, and small grants that prove public value.
